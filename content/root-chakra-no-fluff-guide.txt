## I Couldn’t Sit Still

For months I was the person who left parties early, who said no to trips, who lay in bed with my heart racing for no reason. I told myself I was "just stressed." Then someone mentioned the root chakra — Muladhara — and described it as your foundation: safety, survival, belonging. I thought, *I don’t feel safe anywhere.* Not in my body, not in my apartment, not in my own skin. That was the first time I connected the word "chakra" to something I could actually feel.

I’m not here to sell you on energy anatomy. I’m here to say that the *felt sense* of being grounded, safe, and allowed to exist is real. The root chakra is just a name for that. When it’s shaky, life feels shaky. When it’s steady, you have a base to build on. This is a no-fluff guide to that base.

> When the root is strong, the tree can grow toward the sky.
> — from yogic teaching on Muladhara

---

## What the Root Chakra Is (Without the Jargon)

In the yogic map, **Muladhara** — the root chakra — sits at the base of the spine, in the pelvic floor and the space between the anus and genitals. It’s associated with the earth element, the color red, and the sense of *I am here, I am allowed to be here, I have a right to exist.* It’s not abstract. It shows up as: Do I feel safe in my body? Can I rest? Do I trust that my basic needs will be met? Do I belong somewhere?

When the root is strong, you feel grounded, stable, and able to handle life’s bumps. When it’s weak or blocked — from trauma, chronic stress, moving a lot, financial fear, or early experiences of unsafety — you might feel anxious, scattered, disconnected from your body, or like you’re never quite "home." You might overwork, over-consume, or cling to people or things for a sense of security. None of that makes you broken. It makes you human. And it’s workable.

---

## Signs Your Root Might Need Attention

You don’t need a diagnosis. You just need honesty with yourself. See if any of this fits:

- **Chronic anxiety** — especially free-floating, "something’s wrong" energy with no clear cause
- **Trouble resting** — you can’t switch off, or you feel guilty when you do
- **Feeling like you don’t belong** — in your family, your job, your city, or your own life
- **Body disconnection** — living in your head, ignoring hunger or exhaustion, or feeling numb from the neck down
- **Financial obsession or fear** — money dominates your thoughts even when you’re okay
- **Rigidity or over-control** — you need everything planned, and change throws you

None of this means "your root chakra is broken." It means your nervous system and your psyche might be asking for more safety. The practices below are ways to answer that.

::callout icon=🩺 title=When to Get Support
If your anxiety or sense of unsafety is overwhelming, or if you have a history of trauma, please work with a therapist or a somatic practitioner. Chakra work can support healing; it doesn’t replace professional care. You deserve to feel safe, and sometimes that takes a team.
::end

## Practices That Actually Help

These are the things that moved the needle for me. No crystals required — though if a red stone on your desk reminds you to breathe, use it.

### Put Your Body on the Ground

- Sit or lie on the floor. Feel your weight. Let the earth (or the floor) hold you. You’re not holding yourself up; something solid is under you. Do this for at least five minutes a day. It sounds too simple. It isn’t.

### Breathe Into the Base

- On the inhale, imagine the breath going down into your belly, your hips, your sit bones, the base of your spine. On the exhale, imagine any fear or tension leaving through your roots into the ground. You don’t have to "see" anything. Just direct the breath and the attention down.

### Move Your Pelvis and Legs

- Walk, squat, stretch your hips, dance. The root lives in the lower body. When you move that area, you’re signaling to your nervous system that this part of you is here and allowed to take up space.

### Eat and Sleep Like You Matter

- Skipping meals or burning the candle at both ends sends a message: "My needs don’t count." The root chakra is about basic needs. Feed yourself. Rest. You’re not being lazy; you’re building a foundation.

### Name One Thing That’s Stable

- When you’re spiraling, name one thing that’s true and steady right now: "The floor is under me." "I have a roof." "My heart is beating." You’re not denying the chaos. You’re reminding your system that not everything is lost.

## The Hard Part

**Safety isn’t something you achieve once.** Life will shake you again. The root isn’t about never being scared. It’s about having a place to return to — a body that can land, a breath that can go down, a moment where you remember you’re allowed to be here. Some days you’ll feel it. Some days you won’t. Keep coming back to the practices. You’re not fixing yourself. You’re building a relationship with your own foundation.

---

## You Have a Right to Be Here

The root chakra, in plain language, is about one question: *Do I have a right to exist?* Not to achieve, not to be perfect — to exist. To take up space. To have needs. To be here. If that question has ever felt hard for you, you’re not alone. And you can work with it — not with magic, but with attention, breath, and the repeated message to your body and mind: you are allowed to be here. The ground is under you. You can rest.

That’s the whole practice. The rest is detail.
